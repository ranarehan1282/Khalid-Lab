Protein Dynamics  ·  Structural Biology  ·  Bioinformatics

We are a computational research group at Quaid-i-Azam University Islamabad led by Dr. Rana Rehan Khalid. Our work combines NMR-guided protein dynamics, cryo-EM modelling, AI-assisted prediction, and structure-based drug discovery to address fundamental questions in structural biology and human disease.

Research Areas

Protein Dynamics & NMR

Characterising conformational heterogeneity, allosteric communication, and folding in signalling proteins using MD simulations, NMR spectroscopy, and SAXS data.

Mutation & Disease Analysis

Predicting structural and functional consequences of somatic mutations — including cancer-linked and retinitis pigmentosa variants — in proteins such as PDE6 and PKG-Iα.

Structure-Based Drug Discovery

E-pharmacophore modelling, virtual screening, and MD-based rescoring to identify novel inhibitors for cardiovascular, oncological, and parasitic disease targets.

AI-Assisted Bioinformatics

Developing AI pipelines for chemical shift prediction, CSF proteomics biomarker discovery in Alzheimer's disease, and structural annotation of hypothetical proteins.
