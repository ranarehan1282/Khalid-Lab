Research

Exploring protein dynamics, disease mechanisms, and structure-based therapeutics through computational modelling, bioinformatics, and AI.

Protein Dynamics & NMR Spectroscopy

Understanding how proteins move is as important as understanding their structure. We use molecular dynamics (MD) simulations, complemented by solution NMR spectroscopy and small-angle X-ray scattering (SAXS), to characterise the conformational landscapes of signalling proteins. A central focus is human soluble guanylate cyclase (hsGC) — the primary receptor for nitric oxide (NO) — whose activation triggers vasodilation. We have built integrative cryo-EM–guided full-length models of hsGC and characterised inter-domain cross-talk upon NO binding.

We also study the heme nitric oxide/oxygen (HNOX) domain under diatomic gaseous ligands (NO, CO, O₂), mapping how each ligand reshapes the domain's conformational ensemble and influences downstream signalling.

Mutation Analysis & Disease Mechanisms

Point mutations can have profound consequences on protein stability, function, and interactions. We apply integrative computational approaches — homology modelling, normal mode analysis, free energy perturbation, and MD — to understand how somatic mutations drive disease.

Current projects include:

  • PDE6 mutations in retinitis pigmentosa and cancer: We mapped the three-dimensional organisation of human cGMP-specific phosphodiesterase 6 (PDE6) and interpreted the structural consequences of >100 somatic mutations identified in cancer and inherited retinal dystrophy datasets.
  • PKG-Iα autoinhibition: We characterised the structural basis of conformational heterogeneity and autoinhibitory mechanisms in the catalytic domain of human cGMP-specific protein kinase Iα (PKG-Iα), a key cardiovascular signalling node.
  • PON1 in familial hypercholesterolaemia: Collaborating with clinical partners, we linked decreased serum arylesterase activity of PON1 to LDLR mutations in familial hypercholesterolaemia patients.

Structure-Based Drug Discovery

We develop and apply end-to-end computational pipelines for hit identification and lead optimisation, targeting proteins with validated therapeutic relevance.

Ongoing efforts include:

  • Cardiovascular targets (PDE5A / PDE3A): Using e-pharmacophore modelling and large-scale virtual screening to identify dual-specific PDE5A/PDE3A inhibitors as potential anti-hypertensive leads.
  • hsGC allosteric modulators: A recursive docking pipeline coupled with energy-based pose rescoring and MD identifies allosteric modulators of the βH-NOX domain — an underexploited therapeutic site for cardiovascular disease.
  • Anti-dengue compounds: Computational screening of natural product libraries against all four dengue virus serotypes identified potent pan-serotype inhibitors, with results validated against experimental activity data.
  • PARP1 inhibitors for oncology: Leveraging shape-based screening and MD simulations to optimise PARP1-specific chemo/radio-potentiators for antitumour applications.

AI-Assisted Bioinformatics & Proteomics

We are increasingly integrating machine learning and AI into our structural biology workflows. Current directions include:

  • Alzheimer's disease biomarkers: As part of a multi-cohort international consortium, we apply proteomics and machine learning to cerebrospinal fluid data to identify reproducible molecular signatures that span asymptomatic to symptomatic stages of Alzheimer's disease.
  • Chemical shift prediction: AI-assisted pipelines for backbone and side-chain chemical shift prediction from 3D protein structures, with applications in NMR assignment and structure validation.
  • Functional annotation: Homology-based and deep-learning approaches for annotating hypothetical and uncharacterised proteins in bacterial and parasitic genomes.
